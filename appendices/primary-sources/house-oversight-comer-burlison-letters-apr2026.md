## Content

House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform Chairman James Comer (R-KY) and Subcommittee on Economic Growth, Energy Policy, and Regulatory Affairs Chairman Eric Burlison (R-MO) sent letters to the heads of four federal agencies seeking information about scientists and personnel connected to U.S. nuclear secrets or rocket technology who have died or mysteriously vanished. The agencies are:

1. Department of Energy
2. Department of War (formerly DOD)
3. Federal Bureau of Investigation (FBI)
4. National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)

The letters specifically reference the following cases: Michael David Hicks (JPL, died 2023), Monica Reza (JPL, missing June 2025), William Neil McCasland (retired USAF, missing February 2026), and others affiliated with NASA JPL, Los Alamos National Laboratory, MIT, and a nuclear weapons component production facility.

The committee chairs requested a briefing no later than Monday, April 27, 2026.

Rep. Burlison stated publicly: "We are in competition with China, Russia, and Iran on nuclear technology, advanced weapons, and space. Meanwhile, our top scientists keep vanishing. This has all the hallmarks of a foreign operation." He also stated: "I am working with Democrats and Republicans to get the FBI fully engaged. This is not partisan." Burlison told Fox News that foreign intelligence operatives targeting U.S. scientists would be "the most likely explanation."

## Content

MIT President Sally Kornbluth announced the death of Professor Nuno Loureiro, director of the Plasma Science and Fusion Center, who died early on December 16, 2025, from gunshot wounds sustained hours earlier at his Brookline, Massachusetts residence on the evening of December 15.

Kornbluth stated: "Professor Nuno Loureiro, director of the Plasma Science and Fusion Center (PSFC), died early this morning from gunshot wounds he sustained a few hours before. In the face of this shocking loss, our hearts go out to his wife and their family and to his many devoted students, friends and colleagues."

The statement noted Loureiro's biography: born in central Portugal, physics degree from Instituto Superior Tecnico in Lisbon, PhD from Imperial College London, postdoctoral research at Princeton Plasma Physics Laboratory and UK Culham Centre for Fusion Energy. He joined MIT faculty in 2016, received tenure in 2017, held joint appointments in nuclear science/engineering and physics, became PSFC deputy director in 2022 and director in May 2024.

Loureiro had stated: "Fusion energy will change the course of human history."

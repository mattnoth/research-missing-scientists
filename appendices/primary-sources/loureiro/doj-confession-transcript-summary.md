# DOJ Video Confession Transcript Release

**Source:** U.S. Attorney's Office for the District of Massachusetts / Department of Justice
**Date released:** January 6, 2026
**Date accessed (via media):** April 20, 2026
**Tier:** T1 (federal law enforcement primary source)
**Confidence:** Confirmed
**Note:** Direct DOJ release page not fetched; details extracted from multiple media reports of the transcript (Boston 25 News, ABC News, PBS, Fox News, CBS News). All major outlets reported consistent details from the same DOJ release.

## Key Facts Extracted

- Videos were recorded by Claudio Manuel Neves Valente on an electronic device found during federal search warrant execution on December 18, 2025, at a storage facility in Salem, New Hampshire
- Videos were recorded in Portuguese and translated to English
- Valente confessed to planning a shooting at Brown University "for at least six semesters"
- He said he had "carefully canvassed Brown's campus" and had "plenty of opportunities, especially this semester" but "always chickened out"
- He wanted to target "a regular room" rather than an auditorium
- On the Loureiro murder: he mentioned only a self-inflicted eye injury during that shooting; no direct quotes about Loureiro specifically were reported
- He expressed no remorse, stating he did not "regret what I did"
- He stated he had "absolutely no patience" for creating a manifesto
- He claimed to be "sane"
- Regarding America: "I have no hatred...I also have no love for it"
- His stated "objective" was to leave "on my own terms"
- Two guns found on his body; ballistics linked one firearm to both shootings
- Autopsy determined death by self-inflicted gunshot wound; estimated deceased approximately two days before discovery

## DOJ Assessment
The U.S. Attorney's Office stated that "based on an initial review of the evidence, Neves Valente did not provide a [clear] motive" in the recorded videos. However, separate law enforcement sources described "20-year grudges" as the driving motivation (reported by ABC News).

## Significance
This is the most authoritative source on the suspect's own account of his actions. The absence of an explicit stated motive, combined with the law enforcement characterization of a "20-year grudge," creates a slight tension in the record. The confession confirms premeditation over multiple years for the Brown attack. The Loureiro murder appears to have been planned separately as a targeted personal attack.

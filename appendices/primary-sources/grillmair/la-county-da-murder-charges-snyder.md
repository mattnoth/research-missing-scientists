## Content

On February 18, 2026, the Los Angeles County District Attorney's Office charged Freddy Snyder, 29, of Llano, California, with multiple felonies in connection with the killing of Caltech astrophysicist Carl Grillmair, 67:

- Murder
- Carjacking (of his own relative, same day)
- Burglary (of a separate home, same day)
- Allegation of personal and intentional discharge of a firearm causing great bodily injury

Bail was set at $2 million.

Grillmair was found shot to death on the porch of his home at the 30700 block of 165th Street East in Llano on the morning of February 16, 2026. The shooting was reported at 6:10 AM. The LA County Medical Examiner determined the cause of death as a gunshot wound to the torso.

LA County Sheriff's detectives determined the two men did not know each other, and no motive for the shooting has been disclosed. Snyder had been arrested in Palmdale on December 20, 2025, on suspicion of carrying a loaded firearm in a personal vehicle — and had previously trespassed on Grillmair's property while carrying a rifle. The prior firearms charge was dismissed for unknown reasons.

Snyder's arraignment was rescheduled to April 29, 2026.

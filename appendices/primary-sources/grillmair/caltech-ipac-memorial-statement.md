## Content

Caltech's IPAC (Infrared Processing and Analysis Center) issued statements mourning the death of Carl Grillmair, who had spent nearly three decades at IPAC beginning in 1997.

Tom Greene, IPAC Executive Director, stated: "He was part of IPAC's bedrock for many years, and his passing impacts all of us across IPAC."

Sergio Fajardo-Acosta, IPAC Astronomer, stated: "It was always a pleasure to experience Carl's creativity in doing science. His methods on exoplanet and galactic structure studies were truly detective work."

Career highlights noted: discovery of dozens of stellar streams, lead author of a 2007 landmark study identifying molecules in exoplanet atmospheres for the first time, work with Spitzer Space Telescope, NEOWISE mission, and NASA Near-Earth Object Surveyor, 147 peer-reviewed papers, and the 2011 NASA Exceptional Scientific Achievement Medal.

Colleagues dedicated IPAC's weekly science talk series, "The Next Seven Minutes," to his memory.

# NMSP Statements on Melissa Casias Case

**Source type:** Tier 1 (Law enforcement statements via media)
**Accessed:** 2026-04-20
**Note:** No standalone NMSP press release was located on the NMSP website. These statements are extracted from T3 news reporting where NMSP officials are directly quoted.

## Spokesperson Wilson Silver (August 2025)

Via ABQ Journal (Aug 26, 2025): NMSP spokesperson Wilson Silver wrote in an email that "We have no updates" regarding the Casias case, two months after her disappearance.

**Source URL:** https://www.abqjournal.com/news/taos-county-woman-lanl-employee-missing-for-two-months/497899

## NMSP General Position (July 2025)

Via Taos News (July 23, 2025) and Santa Fe New Mexican (early July 2025): NMSP stated it is "looking into every angle of this investigation." Neither foul play nor voluntary departure has been ruled out. Casias is classified as "missing, endangered."

**Source URLs:**
- https://www.taosnews.com/public-safety/state-police-consider-every-angle-of-melissa-casias-disappearance/article_c218ed80-fc97-531d-ae54-d84c83fe9efd.html
- https://www.santafenewmexican.com/news/local_news/state-police-investigates-disappearance-of-lanl-worker-from-ranchos-de-taos/article_5e975bb9-e5d2-4853-8368-06504c936020.html

## What NMSP Has NOT Said Publicly

- NMSP has not publicly confirmed or commented on the factory-reset phones. That detail originates from family member Jazmin McMillen.
- NMSP has not named any persons of interest or suspects.
- NMSP has not issued a standalone press release that was locatable online.

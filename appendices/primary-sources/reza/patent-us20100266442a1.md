## Abstract (paraphrased)

Describes burn-resistant metal alloys with high tensile strength for use in oxygen-enriched environments. Composition: approximately 55-75 wt% nickel, 12-17 wt% cobalt, 4-16 wt% chromium, 1-4 wt% aluminum, 1-4 wt% titanium, with minor amounts of carbon, boron, and zirconium.

## Performance Specifications

- Tensile strength: minimum 145,000 psi (optimal ~170 ksi)
- Burn resistance (Extinguishing Threshold Pressure): minimum 4,000-6,000 psi

## Intended Applications

Rocket engine components: pre-burners, turbine disks, turbine housings, ducts, hot gas manifolds, main injector assemblies operating in high-pressure, oxygen-rich environments up to approximately 1200 degrees F.

## Significance

This patent covers the alloy commercially known as Mondaloy (variants Mondaloy 100 and Mondaloy 200). The alloy was used in approximately 12 components of Aerojet Rocketdyne's AR1 engine and the Hydrocarbon Boost Technology Demonstrator. It represents a key technology for U.S. rocket engine programs aiming to reduce dependence on Russian-made RD-180 engines.

## Notes

The patent is assigned to the individual inventors, not to Rocketdyne or any corporate entity. Co-inventor Dallis Ann Hardwick died in 2015.

# Known Unknowns

Gaps where primary sources could not be located or where key facts remain unverified. Organized by case-specific gaps and cross-case analytical gaps.

---

## Case-Specific Gaps

### Garcia: KCNSC employment unverified
- **What we need:** Official confirmation (or denial) from KCNSC, Honeywell FM&T, NNSA, or APD that Steven Garcia was employed at or contracted to the Kansas City National Security Campus.
- **What we searched:** 15 web searches across news outlets, government databases, and institutional sites. Fetched NM DPS record, Newsweek, CBS, Fox, British Brief, Daily Wire, Newsweek FBI overview. No KCNSC or Honeywell statement found. Newsweek reported reaching out to KCNSC with no documented response.
- **Status:** Open. The KCNSC claim rests on a single anonymous source speaking to the Daily Mail. No independent verification has been published by any outlet. This is the single most important unresolved factual question for the Garcia case and materially affects the strength of his inclusion in the cluster.

### Garcia: No APD press release or Silver Alert located
- **What we need:** Official APD press release, media advisory, or Silver Alert for Steven Garcia's disappearance on August 28, 2025.
- **What we searched:** Searched for APD press releases, Silver Alerts, and police reports. Found NM DPS database listing (confirms APD as reporting agency) but no standalone APD document. APD statements in secondary reporting appear to come from media inquiries, not published releases.
- **Status:** Open. APD Records Division may have documents obtainable via records request (505-768-2020).

### Garcia: NNSA statement original text not located
- **What we need:** Full text, date, and medium of the NNSA statement acknowledging awareness of reports about missing employees.
- **What we searched:** Searched NNSA and KCNSC sites; found the statement only as quoted in secondary sources (Newsweek, CBS News). No standalone press release on nnsa.energy.gov or kcnsc.doe.gov.
- **Status:** Open.

### Garcia: No family statements found
- **What we need:** Any public statement from Garcia's family members about his disappearance.
- **What we searched:** All 15 searches and 11 page fetches. No family member is quoted in any located coverage.
- **Status:** Open.

### Chavez: Specific role at LANL
- **What we need:** Job title, division, or functional area for Chavez at LANL. Whether he was a scientist, engineer, technician, or support staff. Whether he held a security clearance and at what level.
- **What we searched:** All available public sources including NM DPS, LAPD notices, local media, and national coverage. No source provides a specific role.
- **Status:** Open. This is critical for assessing whether Chavez's disappearance fits the "missing scientist" pattern or whether he was a non-technical employee.

### Chavez: NM DPS case entry date anomaly

- **What we searched:** NM DPS database record reviewed. No explanation found in any source.
- **Status:** Open. Could be data entry error or could indicate a prior missing person report.

## Cross-Case Analytical Gaps

### Base-rate analysis not performed
- **What we need:** Actuarial analysis comparing the observed rate of deaths/disappearances among defense/aerospace-connected scientists (11 events, ~4-year window) against the expected rate for a comparably sized professional population. Denominator should include the total workforce of LANL, JPL, Sandia, KCNSC, MIT PSFC, Kirtland AFB-adjacent entities, and major aerospace contractors.
- **What we searched:** No formal base-rate analysis was found in any source -- media, academic, or government.
- **Status:** Open. This is the most important analytical gap in the entire research. The null hypothesis (H1) cannot be properly evaluated without it. NTI's Roecker and CSIS's Rodgers made qualitative arguments about base rates but did not provide quantitative analysis.

### FBI investigation findings
- **What we need:** Results of the FBI-led "holistic review" announced in April 2026. Whether the FBI identified connections between cases, classified-access commonalities, or foreign-actor involvement.
- **What we searched:** FBI Director Patel's public statements and FBI written statement to Newsweek. Investigation described as active; no findings disclosed.
- **Status:** Open. FBI stated it would "produce information to the White House and the world." Timeline unclear.

### Congressional briefing results
- **What we need:** Outcome of the House Oversight Committee briefing requested by Comer/Burlison no later than April 27, 2026.
- **What we searched:** As of April 20, 2026 (research date), the briefing has not yet occurred.
- **Status:** Open. Briefing deadline is April 27, 2026.

### Reza-McCasland professional connection
- **What we need:** Evidence confirming or denying a professional relationship between Monica Reza and William McCasland. Both had connections to the AFRL ecosystem (Reza via Aerojet Rocketdyne contract work in 1999; McCasland via AFRL command 2011-2013). WION (Indian media) flagged a "Mondaloy connection."
- **What we searched:** All case files, patent records, media reporting. No documented professional connection found.
- **Status:** Open. This is the most potentially significant unverified cross-case connection.

### Foreign coverage: Daily Mail articles not directly accessible
- **What we need:** Direct URLs and text of Daily Mail articles on the missing scientists story. The Daily Mail is cited by RT, WION, and other foreign outlets as a key early source (particularly regarding intelligence-source claims), but no Daily Mail articles appeared in search results.
- **What we searched:** Multiple searches including site:dailymail.co.uk with various keyword combinations. No results returned.
- **Status:** Open. The Daily Mail's role as a narrative originator for the international story makes this a significant gap.

### Foreign coverage: BBC absence unconfirmed
- **What we need:** Confirmation of whether the BBC has covered the missing scientists story.
- **What we searched:** site:bbc.co.uk and site:bbc.com searches with relevant keywords. No BBC articles found.
- **Status:** Open. The BBC's absence is notable but may reflect editorial caution rather than non-coverage.

### Foreign coverage: Non-English language sources not searched
- **What we need:** Coverage in Russian (TASS, RIA Novosti), Chinese (Xinhua, People's Daily), French (Le Monde), German (Der Spiegel, FAZ), Japanese (NHK, Asahi), Hebrew (Haaretz), Hindi (Dainik Jagran, Amar Ujala), and Arabic (Al Jazeera Arabic).
- **What we searched:** English-language editions only.
- **Status:** Open. Coverage may exist in native languages of outlets whose English editions did not cover the story.

### Inter-case communication or social network overlap
- **What we need:** Whether any of the 11 subjects knew each other personally, communicated, or shared social networks (e.g., professional conferences, alumni networks, community groups).
- **What we searched:** All case files. No documented personal connections between any subjects found.
- **Status:** Open. This is a gap that the FBI investigation is well-positioned to fill using phone records, email analysis, and social network mapping.

### Classified information gap
- **What we need:** Whether any of the subjects were involved in the same classified programs (SAPs, SCI compartments, or restricted data programs). This information would not be publicly available.
- **What we searched:** Public record only. By definition, this gap cannot be filled through open-source research.
- **Status:** Open. Only the FBI/DOE/DOD investigation can address this. If subjects shared access to specific classified programs, it would significantly change the pattern analysis.
